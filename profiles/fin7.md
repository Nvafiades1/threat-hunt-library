FIN7 (Carbanak Group / Carbon Spider)

**Aliases**  
- **FIN7**, **Carbanak**, **Carbon Spider**, **Navigator Group** (some overlap with Carbanak).  
- Under the “Carbanak” moniker, some attribute broader bank heists to them.

**Overview**  
- **Motivation**: Direct theft from financial institutions and payment card data.  
- **Targets**: Primarily financial institutions, restaurants, hospitality, retail, and more.  
- **Geography**: Global, but especially the U.S. and Europe.

### TTPs
1. **Spear Phishing**
   - Highly customized lures, sometimes disguised as legitimate company communications.

2. **Carbanak Malware**
   - A sophisticated backdoor enabling remote surveillance and unauthorized transactions.

3. **POS Compromise**
   - Deployed memory-scraping malware on POS systems.

4. **Direct Bank Theft**
   - Transferring funds from compromised bank networks to offshore accounts (in earlier operations).

5. **Ransomware Partnerships**
   - Evidence suggests collaboration with other financially motivated groups to deploy various ransomware strains.

### Last Known Activity
- **Ongoing**: Despite law enforcement successes, the group rebrands or emerges under new names (e.g., Black Basta, ALPHV affiliates).
- U.S. indictments in 2018 arrested some members, but the network persists.

### Tools & Infrastructure
- **Carbanak**: Banking Trojan/backdoor with extensive RAT capabilities.
- **Cobalt Strike**: Regularly used for lateral movement.
